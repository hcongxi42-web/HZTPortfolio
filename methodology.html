Winsorization: Each characteristic is winsorized at (5%, 95%) monthly to control for extreme values within each cross-section.

Normalization: Cross-sectional rank-normalization to [-1, 1] interval: \(x' = 2 \cdot \frac{x - \min}{\max - \min} - 1\).

Missing value filling: Three-stage strategy — (1) rows with >14 missing features (≈16%) are dropped; (2) stock-specific historical mean imputation; (3) global feature mean as fallback. Post-processing panel: (572,605 × 89), zero missing values.

Backtest Methodology

Portfolio Construction

Expected return for stock i at month t:

\(\hat{r}_{i,t} = \hat{\beta}_{i,t-1} \cdot \hat{\lambda}_{t-1}\)

At each month-end, stocks are sorted into deciles by expected return. The top decile (D10) is held for one month as a long-only portfolio. Monthly rebalancing with a rolling expanding window for λ estimation. λt-1 is the rolling mean of factor returns up to month t-1; βi,t-1 is estimated from the respective model.
