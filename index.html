Abstract

We systematically evaluate Conditional Autoencoder (CA) asset pricing models in China's A-share market from 2020 to 2024. Using 87 firm characteristics across 10 categories, we compare traditional factor models (FF), PCA, IPCA, and CA models with varying network depths (0–3 hidden layers). CA(1-layer) delivers the strongest investment performance: a 5-year cumulative return of +312.1% (vs. CSI 300's +24.2%) after A-share transaction costs, with an annualized Sharpe ratio of 1.30. IPCA-extracted factors align closely with classical value, size, momentum, and liquidity dimensions. Our findings demonstrate that nonlinear conditional factor models offer both superior predictive power and practical investment value in China's retail-dominated, policy-intensive market environment.
